Jeep Unleashes Killer Concept Rigs Ahead of the Epic Easter Jeep Safari

By JeepMe Team • 2 min read

Buckle up, Jeep fam – the annual Easter Jeep Safari is roaring back to Moab, and Jeep just dropped some serious heat with their latest concept vehicles. These aren't your average showroom models; we're talking dialed-in machines built for rock-crawling domination, insane articulation, and that signature Jeep attitude that makes every off-road junkie drool.

As an off-road wrench-turner who's spent more time under Jeeps than on pavement, I can tell you these concepts showcase the kind of mods we dream about: beefed-up suspensions, locking differentials front and rear, massive tire upgrades, and crawl ratios that laugh at steep inclines. Whether you're wheeling a Wrangler or Gladiator, these rigs hint at what's possible when passion meets engineering.

Military-Grade Muscle: Convoy Concept

Leading the charge is the Convoy Concept – a Gladiator transformed into a timeless, military-inspired workhorse. This bad boy channels classic J-Series vibes with modern capability that stands ready for long-haul expeditions.

  • Classic retro front end styling with rugged functionality
  • Enhanced ground clearance and approach angles for Moab's toughest trails
  • Heavy-duty axles and locking differentials for maximum traction
  • Built for torque delivery that conquers anything in its path

Hybrid Overland Beast: Bug Out 4xe Concept

For the green-minded trailblazers, the Bug Out 4xe takes the plug-in hybrid Wrangler platform to new heights. Ultralight materials meet serious overlanding gear for off-grid domination without sacrificing torque or capability.

  • Carbon fiber components slashing weight for better efficiency
  • 4xe hybrid system delivering instant electric torque for silent crawling
  • Integrated camping and overlanding setup with massive storage
  • Impressive articulation and high crawl ratio for technical sections

More Trail-Ready Innovations

The lineup doesn't stop there – expect concepts loaded with Mopar Performance Parts, including Gladiator high-top builds, Rubicon upgrades with sun-chasing vibes, and even teases of a potential two-door truck revival. These rigs push boundaries with enhanced suspension travel, reinforced frames, and modifications that scream 'build me next.'

Moab's red rock playground is the perfect stage for these monsters, proving once again why Jeep owners live for the crawl. These concepts aren't just eye candy – they're blueprints for what we can bolt onto our own rides to level up articulation, torque vectoring, and pure off-road grit.
