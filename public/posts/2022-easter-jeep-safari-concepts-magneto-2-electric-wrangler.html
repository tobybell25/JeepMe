2022 Easter Jeep Safari Concepts: Insane Electric Wrangler Magneto 2.0 Hits 60 MPH in 2 Seconds + Six More Beasts

By JeepMe Team • February 14, 2026

Brothers and sisters of the trail, gather 'round because the 2022 Easter Jeep Safari in Moab unleashed pure off-road adrenaline. Jeep brought seven killer concepts that blend hardcore capability, electric innovation, and that classic Jeep spirit we live for. Leading the charge? The Wrangler Magneto 2.0—an all-electric beast that's rewriting the rules of torque and acceleration while still crawling rocks like a boss.

Jeep Wrangler Magneto 2.0: Electric Thunder on the Rocks

This ain't your grandpa's Wrangler. The Magneto 2.0 takes last year's electric concept and cranks it to eleven. We're talking massive upgrades across the board for blistering performance and legitimate trail dominance. Instant torque delivery means no waiting for boost—this thing lunges forward with authority.

  • Power: 625 horsepower
  • Torque: 850 lb-ft of instant electric grunt
  • 0-60 mph: Claimed 2.0 seconds (yes, you read that right)
  • Powertrain: Custom axial flux electric motor, 6-speed manual transmission, 800-volt system, four lithium-ion battery packs (70 kWh total)
  • Chassis mods: 12-inch wheelbase stretch (hello LJ vibes), 3-inch suspension lift
  • Wheels & Tires: 40-inch rubber for massive articulation
  • Axles: Dynatrac ProRock 60 front, ProRock 80 rear with 5.38:1 gears and locking differentials

Imagine ripping through Moab's slickrock with silent, zero-emission power, then having enough torque to climb vertical faces without breaking a sweat. This concept proves electric can deliver the soul of off-roading—quiet, controllable, and ridiculously capable.

The Rest of the 2022 Easter Jeep Safari Lineup

Jeep didn't stop at one showstopper. The full squad includes electrified and retro-inspired builds that push boundaries:

  • Grand Cherokee Trailhawk 4xe Concept: Plug-in hybrid prowess with enhanced trail hardware for family-friendly adventure.
  • '41 Concept: Military heritage meets modern 4xe tech—a nod to Jeep's WWII roots with tan soft top and rugged styling.
  • Rubicon 20th Anniversary Concept: Celebrating two decades of Rubicon dominance with special badging and upgraded off-road goodies.
  • Gladiator/Wrangler mash-ups and wide-open cabin designs loaded with Mopar performance parts, QR-coded accessories, and pure open-air freedom.

These concepts aren't just eye candy—they're rolling testbeds for locking differentials, high crawl ratios, articulation, and the future of 4x4 torque management. Whether you're building your next Wrangler or Gladiator, there's inspiration here for days.
